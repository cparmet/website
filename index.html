I'm happy to pay for subscriptions to quality news sources, but their websites are filled with so many distractions and attention vampires! It uses a couple large language models (LLMs), real-time APIs, feeds, web scraping to gather and process content. A daily issue can report the latest happenings in your town, state, country, planet, and universe; forecast your local weather; give you a heads up on upcoming events in your area; track stocks of interest; alert you if your favorite NBA or NHL team plays tonight (go Celtics üçÄ!); and get new content such as XKCD comics. A data science tool that saved 1-2 years



I created a tool to help grow an ontology, which is a kind of tree of relationships between concepts in medicine. An example of such a relationship is [insulin] -treats-> [type 2 diabetes]. These relationships are invaluable for powering APIs and search for medical content, but they're laborious and expensive to create because of the expertise required. The tool I built, called Cupid because it makes new relationships üíò, reads thousands of medical documents to find concepts and relationships between them. It proposes statements for doctors to review. Having first drafts generated by a machine saved an estimated 1-2 years compared with the doctors coming up with all these relationships manually. The project educated me about some pitfalls to steer clear of when doing entity recognition on medical text.

  NLP & text mining (spaCy, Regex), Python (Pandas, Numpy), SOLR API, Excel
